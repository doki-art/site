/ 01 — Context

One designer. Five years. A live product.

Salesflow is a B2B SaaS platform for automated LinkedIn outreach — used by sales teams, agencies, and enterprise clients. I joined in 2020 as the sole designer and have owned product design end-to-end ever since: no design manager, no design team, no handoff buffer.

That means research, UX strategy, design systems, prototyping, and dev handoff — all sitting with one person. It also means being embedded across PM, BA, engineering, QA, support, and marketing simultaneously, and making every call without a second designer to pressure-test ideas.

The product is still growing. So is the design practice inside it.

/ 02 — The Work
/ 01

Design System — token architecture from scratch

Built Salesflow's first scalable design system, then led its full rebuild with semantic token architecture using Figma Variables — color, spacing, and typography scales. Championed it to the dev team and CEO: engineering set up Storybook and started the Figma-to-Storybook pipeline. A process story, not just a delivery story.

/ 02

Core product flows — campaign builder, inbox, analytics

Redesigned and iterated the platform's most critical surfaces: multi-step campaign automation builder, inbox at scale, data-heavy dashboards, and reporting modules. Focus throughout: simplify complex automation logic, clarify multi-stage flows, and improve structure where data density creates decision paralysis.

/ 03

Pricing & billing UX — a revenue-critical surface

Stepped in proactively when the team was struggling with the pricing page redesign. Identified requirement gaps before design work started. Scope: full pricing page with seat-based calculator logic, in-app upgrade and downgrade flows, plan comparison UX, and proration edge cases. High cross-functional coordination — product, engineering, finance logic all in the room.

/ 04

AI-assisted workflow — introduced it to the whole team

The team was using AI individually, in silos. I introduced a shared pipeline: tokenized Figma files connected to a Figma MCP server, using Claude to generate and iterate HTML/CSS prototypes from design context — pushing and pulling between design and code. Presented this to the CEO as a systemic initiative, not a personal shortcut. Next step: connect MCP to Mixpanel for data-informed design decisions.

/ 04 — Reflection
What was hardest

Being the only designer means no one to push back on your assumptions. I learned to build that pressure from outside — involving engineers earlier, running ideas past support and sales before prototyping, using analytics as a second opinion. The discipline of designing without a design team is different from designing inside one.

What changed my approach

The AI pipeline shifted how I think about the concept phase. When iteration is cheap, the bottleneck moves upstream — to the judgment call that decides which direction to iterate. That's made me sharper about articulating why before generating what.

What I'd do differently

I would have pushed for a shared design system earlier — and framed it as an engineering speed story, not a design quality story. The pitch that worked for Storybook integration was about dev velocity, not visual consistency. Understanding what the other team needs to hear is its own design problem.
